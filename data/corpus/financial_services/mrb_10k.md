## Item 1A — Risk Factors (excerpt)

### Liquidity and funding risk

A significant portion of our funding is concentrated in interest-bearing deposits from commercial clients in the regional banking sector. As of December 31, 2025, our uninsured deposits represented approximately 38% of total deposits, and a rapid withdrawal of these deposits could require us to sell securities at a loss or borrow at unfavorable rates. We rely on access to wholesale funding markets and the Federal Home Loan Bank for contingent liquidity, and a disruption in those markets could adversely affect our ability to meet obligations as they come due.

### Interest-rate risk

Our net interest margin is sensitive to changes in market interest rates. A sustained inversion of the yield curve compresses the spread between our funding costs and asset yields, which could reduce net interest income in future periods.

## Item 7 — Management's Discussion and Analysis (excerpt)

Total deposits declined 4.1% year over year, driven primarily by attrition of higher-cost commercial accounts. Management has increased on-balance-sheet liquidity to 14.2% of total assets, up from 11.0% in the prior year, to mitigate the funding concentration described in Item 1A. Our Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital ratio was 11.6% at year end, above the regulatory minimum, providing capacity to absorb unexpected losses.
